Working as Level Designer
Initially I built a simplified version of  Mime Time within Game Maker. Using this I built a few different level layouts which we could play through, as to get an early feel for the game. This allowed us to easily try different level designs before the artists began work on the models, as we imagined the different areas fitting to our chosen theme of a Fairground (the central slanted platforms being a Helter Skelter for example). This build also helped uswith early game balancing, unsderstanding how fast the characters should move and how powerful the weapons could be. After we agreed on an initial level layout, I made a blockout of the stage within Maya. This would allow the programmers to begin working with the Mimes in game and give the artists a better understanding of how the models need to be scalled. The final build involved all the assets that the artists built. I was responsible for placing their models within the final map and also setting the spawn locations for the Mimes and Crate objects.
