A  2D death-match game developed in Tengine for PC. Each player controls a group of Mimes and have to take out the opposing team using imaginary weapons such as the Handgun, Eyeper Rifle and Fingernade.

Done in Spring of 2016, a group of 8 had 5 weeks to create the game. My primary roles within the game's development cycle was as Project Manager and Level Designer. As Project Manager I analyised the project as whole, talked with the other members of out team and evaluted what was essential for our finished project. I then broke these up and setup a time table of objectives to reach for each person on a week-by-week basis, reavulating it on the start of each new week. These weekly objectives where also broken down further into their core components and documented in priority order.

Working as Level Designer
Initially I built a simplified version of  Mime Time within Game Maker. Using this I built a few different level layouts which we could play through, as to get an early feel for the game. This allowed us to easily try different level designs before the artists began work on the models, as we imagined the different areas fitting to our chosen theme of a Fairground (the central slanted platforms being a Helter Skelter for example). This build also helped us with early game balancing, unsderstanding how fast the characters should move and how powerful the weapons could be. After we agreed on an initial level layout, I made a blockout of the stage within Maya. This would allow the programmers to begin working with the Mimes in game and give the artists a better understanding of how the models need to be scalled. The final build involved all the assets that the artists built. I was responsible for placing their models within the final map and also setting the spawn locations for the Mimes and Crate objects.
